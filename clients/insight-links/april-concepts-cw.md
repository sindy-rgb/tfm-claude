## DCT_132 — The Professional Filter
**Framework:** Text Callout Static / Pain-Point Amplification (Framework #2) + explicit role-qualifying language
**Newsletter:** Cardiac Wire
**Format:** Static text callout
**Rationale:** This concept doubles down on what is already working. DCT_118 Text Callout is CW's only quality performer ($8.47 qual CPL, 18.5% qual rate) and DCT_124 CardiologistFilter is in the pipeline specifically to improve qualification. DCT_132 merges both approaches: a text callout that explicitly names the target audience and makes the filtering function the core value proposition. The copy is designed to make a non-cardiologist feel like this newsletter is not for them — which is the entire point. The "not for everyone" framing is borrowed from DCT_124's creative-as-filter thesis and grounded in DCT_118's proven text-heavy format. This should be the concept with the highest qualification rate in the batch — designed to push toward 20%+ even if raw CPL is slightly higher.

### Primary Text (3 variants)

1. Cardiac Wire is not for everyone in healthcare. It is for cardiologists.

If your work involves interventional procedures, cardiac imaging, electrophysiology, heart failure management, or structural heart — this is your briefing. Twice a week, one email covering the cardiology research, ACC and ESC updates, and clinical developments that matter to your practice.

If you are not in cardiology, this will not be relevant to you. That is by design. Cardiac Wire is curated specifically for cardiologists and cardiac specialists who need to stay current without adding hours to their week.

39,000+ cardiology professionals subscribe. It is free.

2. This newsletter is written for one audience: cardiologists who need to stay sharp without drowning in journals.

Not nurses. Not general practitioners. Not patients. Cardiologists, cardiac surgeons, electrophysiologists, and cardiology fellows who are responsible for knowing what moved in their field this week.

Cardiac Wire filters the noise — ACC guidelines, ESC updates, key clinical trials, practice-changing research — and delivers it twice a week in one curated email. You are caught up before rounds.

39,000+ cardiology leaders read it. Subscribe free.

3. If you are a cardiologist, you already know this problem: too many studies, too many guidelines, too many sources, and not enough hours to read any of them properly.

Cardiac Wire was built for exactly this. It is a curated briefing — twice a week — covering the cardiology research and clinical developments that a practicing cardiologist actually needs to know. Nothing more. Nothing that wastes your time.

It is not a general healthcare newsletter. It is not for a broad audience. It is for you specifically. 39,000+ of your colleagues already read it.

Subscribe free.

### Headlines (2 variants)
1. Built for cardiologists only
2. Not another healthcare newsletter

### Descriptions (2 variants)
1. Curated for your specialty
2. 39,000+ cardiologists subscribe

### Visual Direction
Black text on white background. The first line is oversized and bold: "This is not for everyone." Below it, in standard body text: "Cardiac Wire is a curated briefing for cardiologists, cardiac surgeons, and electrophysiologists." Then a thin horizontal rule, and below that in slightly smaller text: "Twice a week. One email. Everything in cardiology that matters." No imagery. No design elements. The stark "not for everyone" opener acts as both a hook and a qualification filter — it stops the scroll by being deliberately exclusionary, which signals credibility to the target audience and signals irrelevance to non-targets.

### Targeting Notes
