## Per-Newsletter Deep Dive

### Cardiac Wire (CW) — $2,970 spent, $12.69 qualified CPL

CW is the volume machine — lowest raw CPL ($1.73) across all three newsletters. But it also has the worst qualification rate at 8.1%. For every 12 raw leads, only 1 qualifies. That means 92% of CW leads are wasted from Jake's advertiser perspective.

**Why CW qualification is so low:** The broad-appeal creative (UGC doctor, Life hack) attracts nurses, retired professionals, and patients who are interested in cardiac content but are NOT the cardiologists and cardiac surgeons advertisers pay for. The "Confession" concept at $1.76 raw CPL looks incredible — until you see it converts only 7.5% qualified.

**Best CW ad by qualified CPL:** DCT_118_Text Call out at $8.47. This is CW's only truly performant ad. **Worst CW ad by qualified CPL:** DCT_105_UGC doctor at $110.03. Spent $110 and produced exactly 1 qualified lead. The UGC doctor format attracts curiosity clicks from general audiences. The one qualified lead was an interventional cardiologist — but 50 others were not. Kill immediately.

**CW takeaway:** DCT_118 is carrying CW single-handedly. DCT_103_Confession is the highest-spend ad but its 7.5% qualification rate means $1,274 in spend produced only 95 qualified leads. That is not terrible ($13.41 qual CPL) but not efficient either. Everything else is underperforming on quality.

## Recommendations for April

### Creative Strategy

**1. Build "If you, BUT" variants for CW and IW**
DCT_123 proved the conditional framing works for DHW at $6.41 qualified CPL. The format is essentially a qualification filter in creative form. Adapt for:
- **IW:** "If you work in radiology BUT feel like you're always behind on imaging research, this 5-minute newsletter is for you."
- **CW:** "If you work in cardiology BUT can't keep up with every ACC update, here's what actually matters this week."
The key insight: "BUT" creates a self-selection gate that filters out casual browsers.

**2. Build text-callout variants for IW and DHW**
DCT_118 is CW's only quality performer at $8.47 qualified CPL. The text-heavy format works because it self-selects readers over scrollers. Adapt the same approach for IW and DHW.

**3. Develop 2-3 new IW concepts in the DCT_125+ range**
IW cannot survive on Confession alone. Priority angles:
- "Replace your journal reading" (iterate on DCT_114's success at higher quality)
- "Radiologist peer recommendation" (iterate on DCT_110's 34.6% qual rate)
- "CME-adjacent" framing — position as professional development, not just news

**4. Kill or sunset all Luiz-era CW concepts except DCT_118 and DCT_103**
The original DCT_101-105 batch for CW has collectively poor qualification rates (0.8% to 7.5%). Only DCT_103 survives on volume, and even it is mediocre at 7.5% qualified. DCT_118 (post-Luiz era) is where CW found its quality signal.
