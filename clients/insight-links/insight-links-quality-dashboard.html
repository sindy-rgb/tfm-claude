Bake-Off Context — What We Can and Cannot Measure

Cross-agency quality comparison requires Jake's manual review. Our ICP qualification system only applies to TFM leads because Growletter leads never see the post-subscribe form.

Why We Cannot Compare Quality Across Agencies Using This Data

TFM's signup funnel includes post-subscribe ICP qualification questions (job title, company, role). These populate the QUALIFIED, TITLE, and COMPANY fields in Mailchimp. Growletter's funnel does not include these questions. Their 93%+ empty QUALIFIED fields mean "never assessed" — not "failed quality." Any comparison using TFM-only fields (qualification rate, seniority, title data) is structurally invalid as a cross-agency comparison.

What Jake Has Assessed (March 2026 Slack)

  • IW radiologists: "TFM delivered 12 radiologists for IW, Growletter delivered 0" — Jake's manual review of subscriber lists, not our automated QUALIFIED field
  • Jake's manual assessments are the only valid cross-agency quality benchmark
  • These spot-checks are not continuous or systematic — they represent point-in-time reviews

How to Get Cross-Agency Quality Data

  • Open rate comparison: ESP engagement data (not in this export) would be the fairest quality proxy available for both sources
  • Manual audit: Jake can pull a random sample from each source and manually classify ICP fit
  • Engagement scoring: Click rates, reading time, and conversion events apply equally to both sources
  • Churn rate: Unsubscribe rates by source over time would indicate which agency delivers stickier subscribers

Volume Context for Bake-Off

Growletter has delivered 3.8x TFM's volume (19,925 vs 5,262 subs). GL has been running longer and at higher spend. TFM's campaigns launched at scale in January 2026 and are ramping rapidly — February 2026 alone delivered 2,670 TFM subs (more than TFM's entire pre-2026 total). Volume parity by newsletter varies: TFM is closest to GL on IW (44% of GL's IW volume) and furthest on CW (22% of GL's CW volume).

Insights for April Concepts

Actionable findings from TFM's quality data to inform next month's creative development.
1

Highest-Quality DCT Patterns to Scale

Newsletter-specific creative wins big. DCTs targeting a specific newsletter by name consistently outperform generic ones. DCT_103 Confession [IW] delivers 71.8% qual rate vs. DCT_103 Confession [CW] at 32.6% — same concept, different targeting. DCT_102 Why Read X? [IW] hits 83.3% qual rate. DCT_104 Life Hack [DHW] reaches 66.5%. Action: Every new April concept should have newsletter-specific variants. Prioritize IW and DHW-specific angles where qual rates run 55–83%.

2

IW Creative Concentration Risk

IW depends heavily on DCT_103 Confession [IW] which drives 587 of IW's 1,253 subs (46.8%). If this creative fatigues, IW volume could drop sharply. DCT_114 Replace Journals (123 IW subs, 65.7% qual) is the only viable backup at scale. Action: Develop 2–3 new IW-specific concepts for April to diversify the creative portfolio. Test "confession" variants and new angles simultaneously.

3

CW Quality Gap — DCT_103 and DCT_104 Underperforming

CW has the lowest qual rate of assessed leads (41.0% vs 55% IW and 54% DHW). The two highest-volume CW creatives — DCT_103 Confession [CW] (32.6% qual) and DCT_104 Life Hack [CW] (18.9% qual) — are both dragging quality down. Per Jake's feedback, DCT_103 CW is driving non-target personas. Action: Pause or rework these CW variants. Test CW-specific copy that explicitly references cardiology roles/context to pre-qualify.

4

DHW Is the Hidden Quality Winner

DHW achieves 54.0% qual rate with strong C-Suite/CEO concentration (DCT_104 Life Hack [DHW] has 20 CEOs alone). DCT_123 If You BUT (58.9%), DCT_115 AppleNotes (55.2%), and DCT_102 Why Read X [DHW] (68.6%) all perform above average. Action: DHW concepts are working — increase spend on winning DHW creatives and iterate on the "business leadership" angle that attracts C-Suite.

5

Form Completion Is the Bottleneck (68% Never Assessed)

Only 31.9% of TFM leads complete the post-subscribe ICP form. The 3,584 "never assessed" leads are a blind spot — many could be qualified but never filled the form. This matters for Jake's perception: if he judges TFM quality only by the QUALIFIED field, he's seeing 828/5,262 (15.7%) when the real rate among assessed leads is 49.3%. Action: Work with Jake to improve form completion rate, or supplement with ESP engagement data as a proxy for quality among non-respondents.

6

Kill List: DCTs Burning Budget on Non-Targets

DCT_105 UGC Doctor [CW]: 129 subs, 2.9% qual rate — only 1 qualified lead. Immediate pause candidate.
DCT_104 Life Hack [CW]: 785 subs, 18.9% qual rate — high volume but low quality for CW.
DCT_101 Reasons Why [CW]: 236 subs, 25.4% qual rate — below threshold.
Social (organic): 21 subs, 0% qual rate — 16 of 16 assessed were not qualified.
Action: Reallocate spend from these to proven IW/DHW-specific creatives and new CW concepts.
