## 3. BRAND VOICE RULES

**NEVER Rules:**
- NEVER use overly casual or consumer-grade language — this audience is medical professionals (MDs, PhDs, executives). They react negatively to anything that feels "fluffy" or oversimplified.
- NEVER make clinical claims or promise specific patient outcomes directly — position the newsletter as information access, not clinical advice.
- NEVER use clickbait-style hooks that feel like spam — clinicians are "allergic to inbox clutter." The worst thing creative can resemble is a hospital-wide blast email.
- NEVER use "AI-generated" sounding copy — Jake flagged wanting natural voice. The brand promise is "written in human voice" vs. legacy medical journals.
- NEVER target or speak to patients, retired professionals, or students — these are non-qualified audiences that have been an ongoing problem.

**Approved Language / Voice:**
- Professional but accessible — "easy and accessible" vs. dense medical journal language
- Time-saving framing: "stay current," "filter the noise," "caught up fast," "smarter while saving time"
- Authority positioning: "curated," "context for smarter decisions," "what actually matters"
- Voice-of-customer phrases validated by Reddit mining: "maze of information," "higher yield," "so much junk," "I can never catch up," "waste everyone's time"
- Confession-style hooks work because they feel authentic and peer-to-peer
- Text-heavy ads outperform visually complex creative for this audience

**Content Pillars (from onboarding):**
1. All the news they need — readers are caught up and can do their jobs better
2. Succinct — they get caught up fast (smarter, while saving time)
3. Written in human voice — legacy news and medical journals are hard to read; their news is easy and accessible
4. Diverse news — covers all important clinical, research, and business news

**Quote on Failed Copy (from OneSheet research):** *"Summaries worry me unless I know they're done by people who understand the field."* — Reddit audience research. This means all creative must signal credibility and domain expertise, not just convenience.

## 4. WINNING CREATIVE SIGNALS

**Format #1: Confession-Style TOV (Text Over Video / UGC)**
- DCT_103_Luiz_Confession — Dominant performer across ALL three newsletters since January 2026
  - Cardiac Wire: 211 leads | $2.01 CPL | 3.30% CTR | 8 qualified (most recent week)
  - Imaging Wire: 81 leads | $3.58 CPL | 2.78% CTR | 7 qualified
- Hook pattern: "Confession: If you're a [role] who wants to [dream outcome], you need to [action]."
- Why it works: Feels peer-to-peer, addresses time scarcity, positions newsletter as the filter against noise
- Client reaction: No fatigue signals flagged. Jake has not pushed back on this format. Bi-weekly notes confirm "Confession is still king."

**Format #2: Life Hack TOV**
- DCT_104_Luiz_Life hack — Strong #2 performer
  - Digital Health Wire: 58 leads | $2.72 CPL | 2.35% CTR | 3 qualified
  - Cardiac Wire: 78 leads | $1.49 CPL | 3.95% CTR
- Hook pattern: "Life hack for [role] trying to stay current on [specialty]"
- Why it works: Frames staying current as achievable with minimal effort; appeals to time-strapped executives

**Format #3: Transformation / Before-After Static**
- DCT_115_AppleNotesTransformation — Strong debut Feb 20, rapidly becoming a top performer
  - Digital Health Wire: 98 leads | $3.65 CPL | 1.92% CTR | 9 qualified (82% qualification rate)
- DCT_114_ReplaceJournals — Best CPL in the Imaging Wire account
  - Imaging Wire: 70 leads | $2.59 CPL | 3.56% CTR | 6 qualified (75% qualification rate)
- Why it works: Visual proof of value; "replace traditional journals" angle resonates with the failed-solutions pain point

**Key Creative Observations:**
- Text-heavy ads qualify more prospects and lead to higher quality audiences
- TOV (text over video) dominates; UGC video shows promise as a secondary format
- Confession-style and Life Hack are the two strongest angle families
- New creatives need minimum 2 full weeks to prove out

4. **Age 65+ leads** — The majority of early leads were 65+, most of whom aren't part of the healthcare workforce. This led to the age targeting fix (25-59). Any creative or targeting that re-opens this gap will trigger concern.
5. **Stale creative without visible iteration** — Jake said about Growletter: "for like six months... they would say, Imaging Wire is still above target, we're going to try some new things and then the next week just on and on."
