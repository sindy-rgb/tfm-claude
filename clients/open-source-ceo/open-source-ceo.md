## 3. BRAND VOICE RULES

### NEVER Rules (Hard Kills)
1. **NEVER** write dry, academic, or "textbook" content — "business content is boring"
2. **NEVER** use dark, brooding, motivational-poster aesthetics — Bill wants fun, colorful, lighthearted
3. **NEVER** sound like a 70-year-old writing about tech
4. **NEVER** produce dense, Lenny's-Newsletter-style purely educational content — OSC is deliberately NOT HBO-level density
5. **NEVER** use overly polished corporate language — Bill's voice is scrappy, bantery, human

### Approved Language & Tone
- **Voice Positioning:** "50% education, 50% entertainment" = EDUTAINMENT
- **Brand Analogy (Bill's own):** OSC is Netflix — "fun, warm and fuzzy, never know what you are going to get"
- **Closest Comparable:** My First Million podcast
- **Memes are core:** Bill specifically requested more meme-focused content
- **Strategic copy shift (Updated March 2026):** Moving from edutainment-only hooks ("stop reading boring essays") to outcome-driven, framework-focused hooks. Emphasize specific frameworks, playbooks, and competitive edge positioning to attract established founders (Series A-D) rather than early-stage wantrepreneurs. Bill agreed this is the right direction given the "incredibly high caliber founders and executives" in the content.
- **Tone of voice checker incoming (Updated March 2026):** Bill wants to build a Claude-based tone-of-voice checker (similar to one used at Athyna) to ensure all OSC ad copy is consistently on-brand. Brand refresh with new colors, fonts, and tone guidelines expected ~2 weeks from Mar 3.
- **No founder-face UGC (Updated March 2026):** Bill prefers to stay behind the scenes. Aubree pivoting to branded UGC style that leads with the newsletter's value prop rather than personal KPIs. No reliance on founder-face content.
