## Brand Voice Rules

**NEVER Rules:**
- NEVER change the name "How to AI" — Ruben: "I will never change 'How to AI', it's perfect."
- NEVER position as a tool list or AI news aggregator — differentiated from The Neuron, TLDR, etc.
- NEVER use replacement fear hooks (e.g., "AI will take your job") — Ruben's philosophy is empowerment, not fear
- NEVER use tool-focused benefit frames — teaches *how to think with AI*, not *what tools exist*
- NEVER use corporate jargon — "Corporate language in = corporate language out"
- NEVER call it a "course" or "program" — it is a free newsletter
- NEVER hard-sell the paid subscription — ad funnel is for the FREE newsletter
- NEVER propose aggressive landing page tagline changes without flagging CVR crash risk (45% to 24% precedent)

**Approved Voice:** Direct, nerdy, practical, anti-hype. "Smart friend giving advice." Not a guru, not a hype man. Think casual expertise.
- **Core tagline:** "Master 'How to AI' with words, not code."
- **Core differentiation:** "They learn what to type, not how to think."
- **Key phrases:** "Stupidly practical AI guides," "Work faster & write better without becoming a prompt engineer"
- No "game-changer," "revolutionary," or breathless tone. No technical jargon — audience is explicitly non-technical.

**Winning Formats:** UGC talking-head (Arcads AI-generated + real UGC), static infographics (LinkedIn content repurposed), direct-question hooks, Reddit/social proof testimonials, text-over-video
