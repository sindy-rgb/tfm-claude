## TASK 4: COMPETITOR RESEARCH

### 1. Point.me

**What it does:** Web-based award flight search engine that searches across 100+ airlines and 30+ programs. Includes step-by-step booking guides and transfer bonus integration.

**Pricing:**
| Tier | Price | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Basic (Free) | $0 | Weekly deals email, points wallet, limited search (15 results, 60 days out) |
| Standard | $10.75/mo ($129/yr) | Unlimited search, connected wallet, advanced filters, booking instructions, 3 alerts |
| Premium | $21.67/mo ($260/yr) | + Strategy consultation ($200 value), $100 concierge credit, 10% concierge discount, 5 alerts |
| Concierge | $200/passenger | Full-service booking with expert itineraries |

**Strengths:** Deepest airline coverage (100+), step-by-step booking instructions, beginner-friendly, transfer bonus integration, partnerships with Amex and Bilt (free access for cardholders)

**Weaknesses:** Very slow (~2 min per search), expensive ($129-260/yr), web-based (not integrated into existing search workflow), phantom availability complaints, no free real-time search

**How Points Path Differentiates:**
- Free vs. $129+/year
- Instant results vs. ~2 min load times
- Native to Google Flights vs. separate website
- Real-time data vs. potential phantom availability

### 2. Seats.aero

**What it does:** Award availability search tool that scans ~24 airline programs every few seconds, showing availability across entire regions. Best for finding premium cabin awards.

**Pricing:**
| Tier | Price | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | Last-minute availability, limited search window |
| Pro | $9.99/mo ($99.99/yr) | Full year search, live searches, upgrade finder, advanced filters, generous alert system |

**Strengths:** Fastest refresh rate in the industry (scans every few seconds), excellent for premium cabins (ANA First, Lufthansa First, Qatar QSuites), strong alert system, reasonable pricing

**Weaknesses:** Cached data causes ~20-25% phantom availability, power-user oriented (not beginner-friendly), doesn't show cash comparisons, requires knowledge of award programs

**How Points Path Differentiates:**
- Works inside Google Flights (existing workflow) vs. separate tool
- Shows cash vs. points comparison (Seats.aero is points-only)
- Free tier covers major US programs
- Designed for casual travelers, not just points enthusiasts
- Real-time verification vs. cached data

### 3. AwardWallet

**What it does:** Loyalty program tracking platform that syncs with 600+ programs, tracks balances, expirations, and helps choose optimal credit cards per merchant.

**Pricing:**
| Tier | Price | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | Basic tracking, limited updates |
| Plus | $49.99/yr | Unlimited updates, expiration alerts (90/60/30/7 day), merchant category lookup, trips feature, ad-free, booking requests |

**Strengths:** Broadest program support (600+), expiration tracking, merchant optimization tool, comprehensive "digital filing cabinet" for rewards

**Weaknesses:** Tracking-focused (not a search/booking tool), doesn't help with actual flight search or booking decisions, no cash vs. points comparison, desktop-heavy experience

**How Points Path Differentiates:**
- Decision-making tool vs. tracking tool (complementary, not competitive)
- Points Path answers "should I use points or cash?" while AwardWallet answers "how many points do I have?"
- Real-time flight search integration vs. balance management
- Different use case — could be used together

### 4. MaxRewards

**What it does:** Mobile app for credit card rewards optimization. Tells you the best card to use per merchant, auto-activates card-linked offers (Amex, Chase, Citi), tracks welcome bonuses.

**Pricing:**
| Tier | Price | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | Basic tracking, card recommendations |
| Gold | $9/mo ($108/yr) | Auto-activation of Amex/Chase/Citi offers, spending dashboard, category analysis |
| Platinum | $20/mo ($240/yr) | All Gold features + premium support |

**Strengths:** Auto-activation of card offers (unique feature), best-card-per-merchant recommendations, welcome bonus tracker, mobile-first

**Weaknesses:** Mobile-only, earning-focused (not redemption-focused), doesn't help with award flight search, no points valuation or comparison

**How Points Path Differentiates:**
- Redemption-focused vs. earning-focused (complementary, not competitive)
- Points Path answers "when to use points" while MaxRewards answers "which card to swipe"
- Desktop browser extension vs. mobile app
- Flight search integration vs. merchant optimization

**How Points Path Differentiates:**
- Browser extension (instant, frictionless) vs. standalone platform requiring account creation
- Integrated into Google Flights vs. separate "exploration" interface
- Decision-making at point of search vs. route exploration tool
- Clean UI vs. cluttered interface

### 6. PointsYeah (from OneSheet competitor analysis)

**How Points Path Differentiates:**
- Clean, minimalist UI vs. ad-heavy, cluttered interface

## KEY INSIGHTS FOR CREATIVE STRATEGY

### Points Path's Unique Advantages for Ad Messaging:
1. **Only tool that shows cash vs. points in Google Flights** — No competitor does this. This is the #1 differentiator.
2. **Zero friction** — No signup, no account, no login. Install and go. Competitors all require accounts.
3. **Free** — Point.me is $129+/yr, Seats.aero Pro is $100/yr. Points Path free tier covers the 5 biggest US programs.
4. **Speed** — Instant overlay vs. 2-minute Point.me searches or cached Seats.aero results.
5. **Where travelers already are** — 78% of travelers start on Google Flights. Points Path meets them there.

### Untapped Ad Angles from Competitor Research:
1. **"Stop paying for what's free"** — Point.me charges $129/yr for something Points Path does for free
2. **"No more phantom flights"** — Seats.aero cached data shows ~20-25% unavailable results
3. **"One click, not 5 tabs"** — 41% of award travelers use 3+ tools; Points Path consolidates
4. **"The only tool that works inside Google Flights"** — Unique integration no competitor has
5. **"Works in 2 seconds, not 2 minutes"** — Direct speed comparison to Point.me

### Competitive Gaps to Exploit:
- None of the major competitors operate as a browser extension inside Google Flights
- All competitors require account creation; Points Path does not
- No competitor provides simultaneous cash vs. points valuation at the point of search
- Subscription fatigue is real (54% of travel app users cancelled a paid sub in 2024) — free positioning is powerful
