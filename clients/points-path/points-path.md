## 3. BRAND VOICE RULES

**NEVER Rules:**
1. NEVER use cluttered or visually busy creative — brand is clean and minimalist
2. NEVER reference cached/outdated data or make unreliable claims — transparency is a core pillar
3. NEVER use generic benefit statements — specific pain-point framing converts dramatically better
4. NEVER name competitors directly in ads — use "generic competitor" framing only
5. NEVER imply complex setup or account creation — instant no-signup access is the key differentiator

**Approved Language & Tone:**
- Direct address / problem identification: "If you have points you never use, you need to hear this"
- Loss aversion hooks: "wasting points," "never use," "wrong way," "leaving money on the table"
- Decision-fatigue framing: "Points or cash? Just use Points Path"
- Authority/proof signals: "Redeeming through the credit card portal often gives you just 1 cent per point — vs. 6-12x through Points Path"
- Conversational, relatable, traveler-first tone — not corporate or financial jargon

## 5. NEGATIVE TRIGGERS

**Patterns to Avoid:**
- Generic benefit statements without specific pain-point anchoring
- Cluttered visuals or busy layouts
- Implying complex setup or account creation
- Naming specific competitors in ad copy
- Positioning Points Path as "just another points hack blog" — it is a decision-making tool
- Pure newsletter signup hooks that do not tee up the extension install downstream
