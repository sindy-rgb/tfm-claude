## 4. COMPETITOR RESEARCH

### Autopilot (joinautopilot.com)
- **What it is:** Automated portfolio tracking and replication app -- copy politician/celebrity stock trades automatically
- **Value Prop:** "Choose a Portfolio. Connect your Brokerage. That's it." Passive investing by following top traders.
- **Pricing:** $29/quarter or $100/year per portfolio. Minimum $500 investment per portfolio.
- **Scale:** 90,000+ investors, $2.4B+ AUM, 3M+ downloads. Pelosi Tracker+ portfolio alone has $454M invested.
- **Signup Flow:** Browse portfolios > link brokerage > automatic trade execution
- **Key Differentiators:** Direct brokerage integration (automated execution, not just alerts), politician/celebrity trader transparency, quantified return metrics
- **Target:** Retail investors wanting passive replication of institutional/political trades
- **Funding:** $8M raised, expanding from SF to NYC
- **Competitive Overlap with Stocks.News:** Both target the politician trade tracking angle. Autopilot goes further with automated execution. Stocks.News advantage: broader feature set beyond just politician trades (scanner, news, social sentiment).
- **Creative Insight:** Autopilot's Pelosi angle has been running 100+ days successfully. Our DCT_119 (Politicians) targets the same desire but positions Stocks.News as the alert layer rather than automated execution.

### Moby (moby.co)
- **What it is:** Stock picking service and investment research platform with newsletter + app
- **Value Prop:** "Invest Smarter" -- curated stock recommendations with AI-powered analysis
- **Pricing:** $29.95/mo or $199.95/yr (promotional pricing frequently available)
- **Scale:** 5M+ free users, 30M+ people using platform for investment decisions
- **Key Features:** 3 new stock picks weekly, real-time insights, financial education tools, portfolio tracking
- **Target:** Retail investors seeking curated research without doing their own analysis
- **Competitive Overlap with Stocks.News:** Both offer curated stock intelligence. Moby is newsletter-first, Stocks.News is app-first. Moby's premium is 3x the price of Stocks.News Basic.
- **Creative Insight:** Moby's massive scale (30M users) validates the market. Stocks.News's $9.99/mo vs. Moby's $29.95/mo is a strong competitive pricing angle already used in ads.

### tastytrade (tastytrade.com)
- **What it is:** Full brokerage platform focused on options and futures trading with educational content
- **Value Prop:** Commission-free stock/ETF trading, $1 options, live human support during market hours
- **Pricing:** No account minimums, no monthly fees. Commission-free stocks/ETFs, $1/contract options (open only), $1/contract futures
- **Newsletter:** Free through tastylive (educational content, market research)
- **Scale:** Major brokerage (backed by IG Group, a publicly traded company)
- **Key Differentiators:** Actual brokerage (execution + education), options-heavy focus, live trader support
- **Target:** Active options/futures traders wanting low-cost execution + education
- **Competitive Overlap with Stocks.News:** Minimal direct overlap. tastytrade is an execution platform; Stocks.News is an intelligence/alert platform. Different funnel stages.
- **Creative Insight:** tastytrade's "education + execution" bundle is a different value prop entirely. Stocks.News should avoid positioning against brokerages and instead lean into "intelligence layer on top of your existing brokerage."

### Morning Brew Markets (brewmarkets.com)
- **What it is:** Daily newsletter and podcast covering market news, stocks, crypto, macro
- **Value Prop:** "Making sense of market moves" -- accessible, entertaining financial journalism
- **Pricing:** Free newsletter
- **Scale:** Part of Morning Brew (4M+ total subscribers across all newsletters); Markets-specific count not disclosed
- **Signup Flow:** Email capture, single field
- **Key Features:** Daily newsletter, weekly podcast (hosted by Ann Berry), webinars, educational explainers
- **Target:** Retail investors and financially-engaged consumers, broader than day traders
- **Competitive Overlap with Stocks.News:** Both deliver market news, but Brew Markets is passive consumption (newsletter) vs. Stocks.News active intelligence (real-time alerts + scanner). Different audience intent.
- **Creative Insight:** Brew Markets targets a more passive, education-seeking audience. Stocks.News targets action-oriented traders. This distinction should be sharpened in ad copy.

### MarketBeat (marketbeat.com)
- **What it is:** Stock market research and analysis platform with free + premium newsletters
- **Value Prop:** Daily analyst upgrades/downgrades summaries, stock screeners, and premium research tools
- **Pricing:**
  - Free: Daily email newsletter with analyst rating summaries
  - MarketBeat All Access: $399/year (30-day free trial)
  - Additional promoted newsletters: $49-$99/month
- **Scale:** 4.3M+ subscribers, bootstrapped to ~$40M/year revenue
- **Key Features:** Analyst consensus data, earnings calendars, stock screeners, dividend data, institutional ownership tracking
- **Target:** Self-directed investors who follow analyst ratings and institutional moves
- **Competitive Overlap with Stocks.News:** Both serve active market participants. MarketBeat is research-heavy (analyst data, fundamental screeners); Stocks.News is alert-heavy (real-time social sentiment, price action). MarketBeat's $399/yr premium vs. Stocks.News's $97/yr is another pricing advantage.
- **Creative Insight:** MarketBeat is also a TFM client. Their GrowJoy competition data (from Notion) shows them spending heavily on Space Stocks and New Year LP angles. The "scanner competitors charge $100-$160/mo" claim used in Stocks.News ads may implicitly reference tools in MarketBeat's tier.

## 6. STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR CREATIVE

### Underutilized Differentiators
1. **Price anchoring against higher tiers:** Stocks.News Pro at $24.99/mo and Elite at $69.99/mo exist but are not mentioned in ads. Showing all tiers creates anchoring that makes $9.99/mo feel like a steal vs. competitors at $30-$400/yr.
2. **Social sentiment tracking:** No competitor in this set offers real-time social mention monitoring. This is genuinely unique and should be featured more visually in ads (show the actual alert experience).
3. **Morningstar partnership:** $250/year standalone value included in subscription -- completely absent from current creative.
4. **Multi-asset coverage:** Stocks, crypto, forex, commodities in one app. Most competitors focus on stocks only.
5. **Paper trading mode:** Reduces risk for new users to try the app without real money. Not featured in any current DCT.

### Competitive Angles to Test
1. **"Autopilot copies their trades. We alert you first."** -- Position against Autopilot's automated approach by emphasizing control and speed.
2. **"MarketBeat charges $399/yr. We charge $97."** -- Direct price comparison for similar scanner/alert functionality.
3. **"30 million people use Moby for picks. Smart traders use Stocks.News for alerts."** -- Position as the action layer vs. the research layer.
4. **"Free to try, $9.99 to keep"** -- Lead with free trial to reduce friction, then anchor against competitor pricing.

### Landing Page Opportunities
- **thefinaltally.com** has zero social proof. Adding the 360K downloads figure, 4.7 star rating, and 1-2 testimonials would likely improve CVR significantly.
- The "hate stock news" positioning on thefinaltally.com contradicts the app name and app positioning. This disconnect may contribute to the LP CVR decline (30% down to 6%).
- Consider testing a direct stocks.news/signup/ page in ads vs. thefinaltally.com to see if brand consistency improves trial CVR.
