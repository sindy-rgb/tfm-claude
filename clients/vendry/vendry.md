## 3. Brand Voice Rules

### NEVER Rules
- **NEVER use AI-generated UGC actors that look obviously AI.** Dan specifically rejected an AI UGC concept: "Dont love it tbh, maybe its because i know its AI (i've seen this guy on a AI UGC creator)" (2025-11-10)
- **NEVER use imagery misaligned with B2B marketing audience.** Dan rejected car-selfie UGC: "Not a fan of the car selfie, don't think it fits well with a B2B brand" (2026-02-15)
- **NEVER position Case Studied as a "news" letter.** Dan specifically killed concept #3 (Want the latest news?): "Not sure this will work -- we aren't a 'newsletter' in that sense also feels misaligned with the fomo angles that have been working" (2025-10-31)
- **NEVER use the old Vendry branding in ads.** Dan said: "can we swap out the Vendry image behind the last 3? We don't really use the Vendry branding" -- use Case Studied branding instead (2025-03-26). New logos provided: case-lockup.svg, case-lockup-night.svg (2025-10-14)
- **NEVER use generic stock clips.** Dan flagged: "i think we've lost what works in my opinion. Nathans video has a very strong UGC look to it that blends in with the feed. These new iterations feel like a jumble of stock clips" (2025-12-04)
- **NEVER create fake press quotes attributed to real publications.** Dan questioned this concept directly (2025-12-01)

### Approved Language & Angles
- **Aspirational / FOMO messaging** is the #1 winning tone: "If you're a CMO, you've probably already heard of Case Studied..."
- **Authority positioning** works well: "$100M brand," insider knowledge, CMO-level insights
- **Dream outcomes:** impressing CMOs, trendsetting, beating competitors, getting recognized
- **Specific brand references** are encouraged (Duolingo, Spikeball, Liquid Death, Jet2, etc.)
- **Short, punchy copy** preferred over long-form
- Dan's beehiiv landing page preferred: vendry.beehiiv.com (not vendry.io for newsletter)
- Dan actively contributes copy ideas and inspo: "In your 20's you'll start a newsletter because you're tired of all the fake 'growth hacks' online"

### Tone
- Professional but not stuffy
- Native/organic feel (Meta's Andromeda update favors unpolished content)
- Memes work: "I know you said memes can do well inside your account"
- Case study / educational angle -- showing the playbooks behind real brands
- B2B audience (CMOs, marketing directors, brand marketers) -- not consumer-facing
