## Brand Voice Rules

**NEVER Rules:**
- NEVER use AI-generated UGC actors that look obviously AI — Dan specifically rejected this: "maybe its because i know its AI (i've seen this guy on a AI UGC creator)"
- NEVER use imagery misaligned with B2B marketing audience — Dan rejected car-selfie UGC: "Not a fan of the car selfie, don't think it fits well with a B2B brand"
- NEVER position Case Studied as a "news" letter — Dan killed this angle: "Not sure this will work -- we aren't a 'newsletter' in that sense"
- NEVER use the old Vendry branding in ads — use Case Studied branding (case-lockup.svg, case-lockup-night.svg). For DR ads, use current Vendry branding.
- NEVER use generic stock clips — Nathan's UGC-style B-roll is the gold standard. Dan: "These new iterations feel like a jumble of stock clips"
- NEVER create fake press quotes attributed to real publications

**Approved Voice:** Professional but not stuffy. Native/organic feel. Aspirational/FOMO messaging is the #1 winning tone. Authority positioning: "$100M brand," CMO-level insights. Short, punchy copy preferred. Memes work. Case study / educational angle showing playbooks behind real brands. B2B audience (CMOs, marketing directors, brand marketers).

**Winning Formats:**
1. Text-Over-Video (Nathan's B-roll) — consistent top performer, gold standard
2. Fake Tweet / iPhone Notes — "Tweet 3" iterations perform well
3. Mini Newsletter / Text-Heavy Images — features actual Case Studied content (Duolingo = highest CTR)
4. "You've Already Heard" Authority Hook — DCT 127 breakout performer
5. UGC (selective) — must feel authentic, match senior-marketer ICP
