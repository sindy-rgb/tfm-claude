## 3. BRAND VOICE RULES

### NEVER Rules
1. **NEVER say "travel hack(s)"** — explicitly banned
2. **NEVER say "free lounge access"** — explicitly banned
3. **NEVER show a physical credit card** at any point
4. **NEVER show a credit card inside a digital phone wallet**
5. **NEVER mention specific credit card brand names**
6. **NEVER use jargon or advanced points terminology** — audience is beginners
7. **NEVER run ads without people in them** — graphic-heavy / abstract formats consistently underperform; Emily: "We're having a very hard time getting traction with any ads that don't have people in them" (Updated March 2026)

### Approved Tone
- Friendly, clear, approachable, educational, non-technical
- "Daily Drop helps people understand points easily — you don't need to be an expert"
- Curiosity-driven: "Most people don't realize..."
- "5 minutes a day" micro-commitment framing
- Couples/husband-oriented messaging resonates strongly with 35-44 demo (Updated March 2026)
- Words like "husband" and "traveling as smart couples" over-index positively (Updated March 2026)

### Copy to Watch (Updated March 2026)
- Credit card language is being rejected by Meta even on previously approved ads. Emily used Claude AI for alternative phrasing. Avoid: "Here's a secret your credit card companies don't want you to know" — try reworded variations instead of resubmitting rejected ads.
