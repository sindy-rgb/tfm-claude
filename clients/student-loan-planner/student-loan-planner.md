## 3. Brand Voice Rules

### NEVER Rules
- **NEVER** overpromise guaranteed loan forgiveness — this is a regulated financial space
- **NEVER** use financial fearmongering or overtly negative tone
- **NEVER** reference political opinions or partisan positions on student loan policy
- **NEVER** use generic "debt help" language — always profession-specific
- **NEVER** position SLP as a lender or servicer — they are an educational and consulting platform
- **NEVER** conflate SLP with government programs — SLP is an independent advisor

### Approved Tone & Language
- **Conversational, professional-specific language** — speak directly to each ICP (dentists, physicians, etc.)
- **Pain -> Relief -> Empowerment arc:** "You're tired of guessing" -> "Here's how to make it manageable" -> "You deserve clarity"
- Position SLP as the **trusted expert simplifying chaos** in student loans
- Repeat key emotional themes: **confusion, anxiety, overpayment, and hope through clarity**
- The ads succeed by **creating clarity during chaos — not by increasing fear**

### Approved Copy Patterns
- "Student Loan Planner is the free weekly newsletter trusted by professionals with six-figure student loan debt."
- "Read Student Loan Planner's free newsletter, made for high-debt, high-income professionals like you."
- "This free newsletter explains student loan strategies in 5 minutes."
- "Luckily, this free newsletter shows you exactly what to do next." (strongest payoff line per performance data)

### Key Messaging Angles (Ranked by Performance)
1. **Forgiveness & Policy Changes** — SAVE, PSLF, IDR confusion (highest performer)
2. **Refinancing Opportunity** — "Sleepy giant" now returning relevance; shifting to primary monetization focus
3. **Debt-to-Income Relief** — making six-figure debt manageable
4. **Professional Identity** — speaking directly to each ICP by name ("While other dentists are guessing...")

### Approved Creative Formats
- Text-over-video (B-roll) — **dominant winning format** across all top performers
- ElevenLabs voiceover
- Static images (memes, "dear email" format, fake tweets)
- UGC testimonial (raw, unpolished — DCT_128 format)

## 5. Negative Triggers

### Patterns to Avoid
- **Generic "debt help" messaging** — always specify the profession; generic debt language underperforms significantly
- **Recycling old winning ads** — older winning ads no longer recover performance when turned back on; this confirms the need for new framing, not recycling
- **High-frequency creative** — dentists hit frequency of 3 in February, signaling creative fatigue; monitor and rotate before frequency exceeds 2.5
- **Mixing refi messaging with forgiveness landing pages** — CVR crashed when March refi creatives drove traffic to non-refi LPs (from $16.87% to $11.64% CVR)
- **Overspending on fatigued ICPs** — Physicians and Dentists can burn budget fast when creative is stale; budgets have been cut from $400-450/day to $200/day
- **50/50 creative splits** — Travis explicitly decided against splitting March between normal and private student loan messaging: "Do not make March a 50/50 creative split"

### Specific Creative Kills / Failures
- February concepts that inflated CPL were quickly paused across Physicians and Dentists
- Optometrist ad sets experienced severe creative fatigue — segment turned OFF entirely in March
- Pharmacists and PTs also turned off due to poor ROAS relative to spend

### Audience Concerns
- **Physicians:** CVR dropped to 6.79-7.06% — lowest of any active ICP; landing page conversion is the main bottleneck, not ad engagement
- **Dentists:** Signs of audience fatigue at frequency 3; CPL at $31+ is 50% above target
- **Small ICPs:** Psychologists permanently off; others (Optometrists, Pharmacists, PTs) off pending next cohort analysis
