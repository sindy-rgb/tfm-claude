## 3. BRAND VOICE RULES

### NEVER Rules (Hard Stops):
1. **NEVER mention specific credit card issuers or card names** — compliance/affiliate guardrail
2. **NEVER use hype-driven or clickbait language** — calm, confident, expert-led tone
3. **NEVER plagiarize or fail to document inspiration sources** — Creative QA SOP mandates this
4. **NEVER use generic personal finance framing** — TPG is about points mastery, not broad personal finance
5. **NEVER overpromise what the newsletter does** — Melanie Shapiro flagged this on DCT 243 (Timely Alert Deal) and DCT 249 (Don't Wait on Airlines). Keep claims grounded and specific. (Updated March 2026)

### Approved Voice & Language:
- **Tone:** Expert-led, authoritative, structured. "Step-by-step mastery." Calm confidence.
- **Positioning:** TPG as the insider source — adds credibility and exclusivity
- **Narrative Structure:** "I didn't know → I learned from TPG → now I win" is the repeat winner arc
- **TPG Staff Content:** Showcase TPG staff expertise and authenticity
- **Points-wasting angles:** Continue to resonate strongly — framing around "you're leaving value on the table" works. (Updated March 2026)

### QA Recurring Issues (Updated March 2026):
- **Subscriber count ("1.5 million"):** Must be verified before every use. Wrong sub counts have been flagged multiple times (QA Log entries #58, #31). Confirm with Louisa or recent approved ads.
- **Value claims (e.g., "less than 1 cent per point"):** Must be defensible for the specific context. Card travel portals typically give 1-1.5cpp; only merchandise/gift cards are under 1cpp.
- **Inspiration source documentation:** Required for every concept per QA SOP. Missing on DCT 148.
- **POV consistency:** Melanie flagged DCT 249 switching from "I" to "you" mid-concept. Keep POV consistent within each ad.
- **Text readability:** White text over photos flagged as hard to read (DCT 243). Ensure contrast.
- **Pacing:** Phone POV format — lines read too fast, especially on hooks (DCT 227 feedback).

## 5. NEGATIVE TRIGGERS

### Patterns to Avoid:
1. **Specific credit card or issuer mentions** — Absolute kill. Compliance requirement.
2. **Low-ROAS creatives running uncapped** — Aggressive spend management required. NEW: $3-4K/month spend cap on all new concepts until ROAS validated. (Updated March 2026)
3. **Carousels** — Explicitly deprioritized
4. **Stale creative running 2+ months without refresh**
5. **Generic "points are great" messaging without a specific angle**
6. **Plagiarized or unattributed inspiration**
7. **Campaign-splitting risk** — Do NOT split into testing/scaling campaigns. Duplicated post IDs perform differently when moved between campaigns. Nathan has seen this historically on Meta. Keep single-campaign structure. (Updated March 2026)
8. **Meta auto-optimization blind spot** — Meta optimizes for cheap CPL, not ROAS. Meta cannot see revenue data. TFM must manually control spend allocation to prevent Meta from funneling budget to low-ROAS ads. (Updated March 2026)
9. **Overpromising newsletter capabilities** — Melanie Shapiro has flagged this on multiple concepts. Keep claims specific and defensible. (Updated March 2026)
