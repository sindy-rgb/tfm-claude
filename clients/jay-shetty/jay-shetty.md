## 2. NORTH STAR METRIC

**Primary KPI:** Cost Per Lead (CPL) — cost per new free newsletter subscriber from Meta
**Target:** CPL <= $3.00
**Quality Definition:** Cost Per Lead minus the revenue generated per lead = actual customer acquisition cost. Subscriber quality is measured by engaged rate, open rate, and verified CTR. "Lead quality matters more than volume."
**Secondary KPIs:** Engagement rate (target ~50%+), open rate (target ~45%+), CVR (conversion rate from click to subscribe), unsubscribe rate (watch for upward trends)

**Best Benchmarks Achieved:**
- CPL as low as $0.60 (Jan 2026, DCT_117 reel) and $0.67 (Dec 2025)
- 75% engaged rate, 63.5% open rate, 4.7% verified CTR (Dec 2025 top performer)
- 50.21% CVR best in account (Jan 2026)

## 5. NEGATIVE TRIGGERS

### Patterns to Avoid
- **Hustle / grind messaging** — Anything resembling "work harder" or "rise and grind" energy is brand-toxic
- **Hype or performance tone** — Ads that feel shouty, salesy, or like an infomercial will fail
- **Oversimplifying Jay to one vertical** — Don't reduce him to "the meditation guy" or "the relationship guy"
- **Gary Vee / bro-culture energy** — Direct opposite of Jay's brand positioning
- **Using unapproved brand assets** — No formal brand guideline kit exists yet

### Specific Kills
- **DCT 105 fatigue watch** — Feb 2026 data showed second consecutive week of CVR decline and unsubscribe rates trending upward
- **Wrong Facebook pages** — Early issue (Nov 2025): ads were running on Jay Shetty Coaching/Genius pages instead of flagship pages

### Quality Red Flags
- Unsubscribe rate trending upward = creative fatigue signal
- CVR declining week-over-week = audience saturation in current targeting
- CPL spikes driven by CPM increases (not creative issues) — distinguish between auction cost increases vs actual creative underperformance
