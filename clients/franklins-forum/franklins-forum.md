## 1. Client Overview

### The Newsletter
Franklin's Forum is a free, weekly newsletter covering what matters at the University of Pennsylvania -- governance, campus climate, leadership, policy, and federal impacts. Written by Penn alumni for Penn alumni, it positions itself as an independent, nonpartisan "institutional diagnostic tool" rather than a news digest. The newsletter uses a consistent structure: issue framing, data/facts, tension/tradeoffs, interpretation ("So what?"), and a secondary roundup ("The Almanac").

- **Website:** franklinsforum.org
- **ESP:** Beehive (shares 1636forum.com domain infrastructure)
- **Cadence:** Weekly
- **Audience:** Penn alumni (290K+ total addressable), faculty, staff, parents, students -- spans all 12 Penn schools
- **Publishing Since:** August 2025

## 3. Brand Voice Rules

### NEVER Rules (from client feedback + OneSheet analysis)

1. **NEVER use sensational or crisis-framing language.** Franklin's Forum is calm, measured, and serious. No outrage, no moral grandstanding.
2. **NEVER use partisan or culture-war language.** The newsletter is explicitly nonpartisan. No political shorthand, no "villains."
3. **NEVER position Franklin's Forum as just a "news roundup" or "community."** Gigi explicitly rejected this framing on 2/27/2026: *"We need to lean more into the angle that we want to make change at Penn. This newsletter isn't just about knowing what is going on. It is about rallying a group of people around a drive for change."*
4. **NEVER use founder-focused or face-to-brand ads.** Gigi: *"I need to think a bit more about having any founder-focused ads. I don't think it's the right vibe or that putting a face to the brand is what we should do (same with UGC video)."* (2/27/2026)
5. **NEVER use AI-sounding or gloomy voiceovers.** Gigi on DCT118: *"I like the visuals and the text, but I don't like the voice. It feels very AI and gloomy, and I don't think it gets the emphasis right. Can we have a real voice? or even a more realistic, upbeat AI voice?"* (3/18/2026)
6. **NEVER over-promise impact or create urgency.** The newsletter's implicit CTA is "Be the kind of person who wants this" -- not "Act now."
7. **NEVER run ads without Penn-specific imagery.** Gigi: *"I think we need to make sure that as many of the ads as possible have imagery of Penn."* (2/27/2026)

### Approved Tone & Language
- **Tone:** Calm, serious, measured, firm when necessary, never emotional
- **Voice posture:** Translator > Watchdog > Commentator
- **Speaks with the institution, not at it**
- **Assumes an intelligent reader who wants nuance**
- **Treats readers like responsible adults, not an audience to rile up**
- **Uses data as moral authority, not emotion**
- **Frames critique as care / pro-Penn stewardship**

### Approved Messaging Framework
- **Concern -> Solution:** Lead with emotional concern about Penn's direction, then position Franklin's Forum as the informed response
- **Clarity -> Understanding:** Explain what Franklin's Forum is and why it matters for Penn stakeholders
- **Mission-driven framing:** "There is a group of Penn alumni who want a better future for the university"
- **Rallying language:** "Have you been frustrated with the direction Penn is headed? You are not alone. Join a group of alumni who are driving change."

### Winning Hook Language
- "There's a growing group of Penn alumni paying attention to where the university is headed."
- "Hi, we're Franklin's Forum."
- Alumni paying attention to Penn's direction
- Clear introduction to Franklin Forum's purpose

## 4. Winning Creative Signals

### Top-Performing Format: Text-Over-Video (TOV)
Text-over-video with Penn campus B-roll is the dominant winning format. Alumni recognize campus locations and feel a personal connection. Clear, direct on-screen text + simple visuals outperform complex or heavily produced creatives.

### Top-Performing Ads (as of 03/19/2026)

| **DCT119** | TOV (Penn Alumni Are Reading) | Concern/Awareness | **$3.34** | Top volume driver. 40 leads in first week. Hook: "There's a growing group of Penn alumni..." |

| **DCT116** | Static/TOV hybrid | Brand Introduction | -- | Gigi: "116 looks great. thank you!" (3/18/2026) |

### Two Core Winning Angles
1. **Concern / Awareness (Penn's Direction)** -- Messaging centered on growing concern among alumni about where the university is headed. Emotional driver + social proof.
2. **Brand Introduction (What Franklin's Forum Is)** -- Clear explanation of the newsletter and its value to Penn alumni. Lowers friction.

### What Drives Performance
- Stronger emotional + concern-based messaging
- Clearer, more direct hooks
- Penn-specific campus imagery (recognizable locations)
- Text-over-video format
- Mission-driven framing (rallying alumni for change, not just informing)
