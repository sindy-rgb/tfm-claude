## Brand Voice Rules

**NEVER Rules:**
- NEVER use sensational or crisis-framing language — Franklin's Forum is calm, measured, and serious
- NEVER use partisan or culture-war language — explicitly nonpartisan, no political shorthand, no "villains"
- NEVER position as just a "news roundup" or "community" — Gigi: "We need to lean more into the angle that we want to make change at Penn. This newsletter isn't just about knowing what is going on. It is about rallying a group of people around a drive for change."
- NEVER use founder-focused or face-to-brand ads — Gigi explicitly rejected this and UGC video
- NEVER use AI-sounding or gloomy voiceovers — Gigi: "It feels very AI and gloomy. Can we have a real voice? or even a more realistic, upbeat AI voice?"
- NEVER over-promise impact or create urgency — the implicit CTA is "Be the kind of person who wants this"
- NEVER run ads without Penn-specific imagery — Gigi: "We need to make sure that as many of the ads as possible have imagery of Penn"

**Approved Voice:** Calm, serious, measured, firm when necessary, never emotional. Translator > Watchdog > Commentator. Speaks with the institution, not at it. Assumes an intelligent reader who wants nuance. Uses data as moral authority, not emotion. Frames critique as care / pro-Penn stewardship.

**Winning Formats:** Text-over-video (TOV) with Penn campus B-roll is the dominant winner. Alumni recognize campus locations and feel personal connection. Clear, direct on-screen text + simple visuals outperform complex creatives.

**Winning Angles:**
1. Concern / Awareness (Penn's Direction) — messaging centered on growing concern among alumni
2. Brand Introduction (What Franklin's Forum Is) — clear explanation of the newsletter and its value
