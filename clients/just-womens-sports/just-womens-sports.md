## 3. Brand Voice Rules

### NEVER Rules (Hard Stops)

1. **NEVER use culture war framing.** JWS is deliberately neutral and inoffensive. No political angles, no "fighting for equality" messaging. From kickoff: *"Newsletter is positioned as 'inoffensive' -- neutral, exhaustive coverage without clickbait or culture war content."*

2. **NEVER use clickbait tone or hot takes.** The entire brand positioning is anti-clickbait. From the handover doc: *"No hot takes, no clickbait. Just real coverage."*

3. **NEVER over-focus on Caitlin Clark or Angel Reese only.** JWS covers ALL women's sports comprehensively. Narrowing to celebrity athletes undermines the brand.

4. **NEVER use "pinkwashing" or lifestyle-only angles.** This is a sports coverage newsletter, not a lifestyle brand. Avoid gendered cliches.

5. **NEVER use overly polished/branded creative.** Authentic > polished is a core creative principle. UGC-style content always outperforms.

6. **NEVER use generic sports newsletter messaging.** Specificity wins -- name leagues (WNBA, NWSL, USWNT), name the newsletter, call out what makes it different.

7. **NEVER use Facebook lead forms.** Direct-to-landing-page only to maintain subscriber quality.

### Approved Language & Tone
- **Neutral, objective, factual** -- like a wire service, not an opinion column
- **"Treat women's sports like real sports"** -- core brand positioning
- **"Understand the business and the game"** -- knowledge angle
- **"Pro-women's sports without moralizing"** -- movement without activism
- **"The ESPN.com equivalent for women's sports"** -- competitor contrast framing
- **"If ESPN covered women's sports this well, you wouldn't need us"** -- approved social proof hook
- **Conversational, direct language** that matches JWS editorial voice
- **"5 minutes to stay informed"** -- efficiency positioning for casual fans
- **"500,000+ fans"** -- social proof with specific subscriber count
- **League-specific callouts:** WNBA, NWSL, USWNT, college hoops, CBA negotiations

### Client Copy Corrections (Exact Quotes)
- John Murray (Oct 14, 2025): *"Two small comments on the first two: First one: it should be 'covering the WNBA, NWSL and more' not 'covering WNBA and NWSL and more'. Second one: should be 'Subscribe for free' (missing 'for' currently). Otherwise they look good!"*
- JWS naming convention: Always "the WNBA" and "the NWSL" (with article "the")

### Audience Personas (for copy targeting)
1. **Casual Loyalists (Primary):** Ages 27-44, busy professionals wanting 5-min daily update. Many new to women's sports (50-60% joined in last 2 years). This is THE growth audience.
2. **Women's Sports Insiders (Secondary):** Ages 35-55, long-time fans. Coaches, teachers, marketers. Want depth and continuity.
3. **Sports Industry (Tertiary):** League offices, team GMs, brand CMOs. Small but influential.

### Audience Demographics
- 82% female (newsletter); 60/40 on social
- 81% college-educated
- 66% HHI over $100k
- 58% under 44; 20% retired
- 67% no children
- 90% do NOT bet on sports
- 90% likely to try brands that partner with JWS

## 4. Winning Creative Signals

### Top Performing Formats
1. **Text-over-video (dominant format):** Direct value prop overlaid on live sports footage. Drives strongest CTR and open rates. Examples: DCT_110, DCT_122.
2. **Lo-fi handwritten/organic stills:** Conversational tone on minimal backgrounds. Volume leader. Example: DCT_121 at $0.72 CPL with 450 leads in one week.
3. **Static branded ads with bold copy:** Apple Notes style, "Please stop" callouts. Strong open rates (74-76%).
4. **UGC podcast-style video:** Tested Jan 2026, strong engagement signal. DCT_119 delivered 5.17% verified CTR.
5. **Meme formats:** Expanded brain, confession-style. DCT_118 hit 75% open rate on confession hook.

## 5. Negative Triggers

### Creative Kills (Patterns That Failed or Must Be Avoided)
1. **DCT_115 (Tweet + b-roll):** Generated volume (244 subs) but had a **3.72% unsubscribe rate** -- the highest in the set. Budget was reallocated away from it. Tweet-style formats with generic sports b-roll attract low-quality subs.

2. **DCT_106 (Me trying to explain stock footage):** High CPL ($2.41) with decent volume but poor efficiency. Meme format didn't translate well here.

3. **Overly polished/branded statics:** Consistently underperform vs. UGC and lo-fi formats. Nathan noted early on: "Creative should avoid overly polished/branded content -- authentic UGC performs best."

4. **Getty Images in paid ads:** Cannot be used -- John confirmed Oct 3, 2025: "We cannot use Getty images for ads, unfortunately."

5. **Culture war or activist framing:** From kickoff notes, the newsletter is positioned as "inoffensive." Togethxr and BRW use "activist/empowerment" angles -- JWS explicitly differentiates by being neutral.

6. **Landing page changes without notification:** CVR dropped from ~45% to 29.91% at one point, suspected to be caused by client-side landing page changes. Always confirm with John before diagnosing CVR issues.

### Client Sensitivity: Communication Preferences
Sindy flagged (Dec 16, 2025): *"Some clients prefer more communication than others, and this one seems to want to be kept in the loop. I think it would be best to flag it anytime we make changes on their account, even temporary ones, we should give them a quick heads-up. Over-communicating in this case helps avoid confusion or mistrust, especially since they seem sensitive to changes happening without context."*

This is a critical note: **Always proactively communicate account changes to John before making them.**

### Technical Pitfalls
- **Exclusion list must stay updated** -- stale lists caused ~40% Meta/Beehiiv reporting discrepancy in Dec 2025
- **Pixel tracking was not initially in GTM** -- required manual implementation by Katelyn at Edition Design
- **Landing page pop-up issue** (Oct 2025) tanked CVR to 25.56%; fixing it immediately recovered performance to 42%+
- **Creative fatigue cycle:** Top performers (especially DCT_110) show saturation after ~4-6 weeks. Verified CTR dropped from 4.80% to 2.35% when DCT_110 fatigued in Jan 2026. Must have fresh concepts ready before fatigue hits.
