## Brand Voice Rules

**NEVER Rules:**
- NEVER use culture war framing — JWS is deliberately neutral and inoffensive. No political angles, no "fighting for equality" messaging.
- NEVER use clickbait tone or hot takes — anti-clickbait is core brand positioning. "No hot takes, no clickbait. Just real coverage."
- NEVER over-focus on Caitlin Clark or Angel Reese only — JWS covers ALL women's sports comprehensively
- NEVER use "pinkwashing" or lifestyle-only angles — this is sports coverage, not a lifestyle brand
- NEVER use overly polished/branded creative — authentic > polished is a core principle. UGC-style always outperforms.
- NEVER use generic sports newsletter messaging — specificity wins. Name leagues (the WNBA, the NWSL, USWNT), name the newsletter.
- NEVER use Facebook lead forms — direct-to-landing-page only for subscriber quality
- NEVER use Getty Images for paid ads — John confirmed Oct 2025

**Approved Voice:** Neutral, objective, factual — like a wire service, not an opinion column. "Treat women's sports like real sports." Pro-women's sports without moralizing. Conversational, direct language. "The ESPN.com equivalent for women's sports." "500,000+ fans." "5 minutes to stay informed."

**Copy Note:** Always "the WNBA" and "the NWSL" (with the article "the"). Always "Subscribe for free" (not "Subscribe free").

**Winning Formats:**
1. Text-over-video (TOV) — direct value prop overlaid on live sports footage
2. Lo-fi handwritten/organic stills — conversational tone on minimal backgrounds (volume leader)
3. Static branded with bold copy — Apple Notes style, "Please stop" callouts
4. UGC podcast-style video — strong engagement signal
5. Meme formats — expanded brain, confession-style
