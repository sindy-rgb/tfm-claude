## Brand Voice Rules

**NEVER Rules:**
- NEVER use the phrase "honest news" — the newsletter is analysis and community, not news
- NEVER position as political or agenda-driven — readers want principled neutrality, no partisan noise
- NEVER target faculty directly — positioning is "for alumni" (faculty may be referenced but not directly targeted)
- NEVER use founder's face/likeness in ads — Allison explicitly banned this; Sindy added to playbook
- NEVER frame the newsletter as a "news roundup" — focus on alumni concerns and desire for institutional change
- NEVER copy text from briefs without exact capitalization — QA issue flagged by client

**Approved Voice:** Calm, grounded, intellectual — not alarmist or performative. Community-oriented but not exclusive-feeling. Concern-driven without being outrage-driven. Direct alumni call-outs improve quality. "Sharp, independent analysis. No spin. No partisan noise." "Harvard, Explained Clearly." "5 minutes weekly. Always free." CTA winner: "Join for free" (beat "Join the community" decisively in DCT_123 testing).

**Winning Formats:** Text-over-video (TOV) is dominant. Static (text-forward, handwritten-style) being tested. Hybrid (Harvard B-roll + hook-driven text) in testing. UGC explored but blocked — no confirmed alumni participant.
