## 3. Brand Voice Rules

### NEVER Rules

1. **NEVER use the phrase "honest news"** -- Mariely confirmed: *"We won't use wording like 'honest news.' That was just placeholder language to show direction."* The newsletter is not positioned as "news" -- it is analysis and community.

2. **NEVER position as political or agenda-driven** -- Core objection from readers: *"This might be political or agenda-driven."* Readers want principled neutrality. Avoid any language that sounds partisan or culture-war adjacent.

3. **NEVER target faculty directly** -- From 2/17 meeting notes: *"Adjust messaging to ensure positioning remains clearly 'for alumni' (faculty may be referenced but not directly targeted)."*

4. **NEVER use founder's face/likeness in ads** -- Allison stated clearly: *"I personally hate any photos or videos of myself so wouldn't film for that one"* -- Sindy flagged this for the playbook: "Please add those details to the Creative Playbook so we never ask again for 'her face'."

5. **NEVER frame the newsletter as a "news roundup"** -- From Franklin's Forum crossover notes: focus on alumni concerns and desire for institutional change, not just curating headlines.

6. **NEVER copy text from briefs without exact capitalization** -- From QA Issue Log: *"Need to update the capitalization of first letters. Was mentioned above as the first point. Please copy the text EXACTLY as it is written in the brief"* -- Humza, 1636 Forum

### Approved Language & Tone

**Core positioning:**
- "Sharp, independent analysis. No spin. No partisan noise."
- "A calm voice in a noisy landscape"
- "Harvard, Explained Clearly"
- "Trusted by 25,000+ Harvard Alumni" (update number as it grows -- now 30K+)
- "5 minutes weekly. Always free."
- "The weekly briefing that cuts through the PR fluff"

**CTA language:**
- "Join for free" -- WINNER over "Join the community" (tested in DCT_123: 46 leads vs. 9 leads, plus stronger alumni subscriber signals)
- "Get the Briefing" -- approved variant
- "Join Free" -- approved variant

**Tone guidelines:**
- Calm, grounded, intellectual -- not alarmist or performative
- Community-oriented but not exclusive-feeling
- Concern-driven without being outrage-driven
- Direct alumni call-outs improve quality: explicit "Harvard alumni" language in hooks

### Audience Positioning
- **Primary:** Harvard alumni (30-70), high-achieving professionals, time-poor, media-skeptical
- **Persona 1 -- "The Re-engaged Observer":** Age 30-55, HLS or GSAS alum, academia/consulting/tech/policy. Wants to understand without being pulled into noise.
- **Persona 2 -- "The Loyal Steward":** Age 45-70, Harvard College or HBS/HKS, senior professional. Deep belief in Harvard's legacy. Views reform as coming from within.
- **Secondary:** Friends/parents of alumni (but these are deprioritized for quality purposes)

## 5. Negative Triggers

### Creative Kills & Patterns to Avoid

1. **Broad targeting without quality filter** -- The account's central problem. Broad targeting brings non-affiliated subscribers who inflate volume but dilute quality. Allison flagged: *"effective CPL for qualified subscribers is closer to $7, which is the real concern."* Decision made to test lookalike-only targeting even though CPL will likely increase.

2. **Ads that attract non-alumni at scale** -- DCT_104, while the highest-volume ad, "may be reaching broader audiences than intended." The emotional hooks work broadly but don't self-select for Harvard affiliation. Future creative must incorporate more explicit Harvard alumni targeting in hooks.

3. **DCT_115 -- turned off** -- Fell below 40% open rate threshold (29.17%). Any ad dropping below 40% open rate is flagged for removal.

4. **"Honest news" framing** -- Explicitly rejected. The newsletter is analysis and community, not news.

5. **Gambling / pay-to-play language** -- An ad was rejected by Meta for "promoting gambling or pay to play." Avoid any language that could trigger this policy flag.

6. **High unsubscribe rates from specific hooks** -- DCT_123 shows strong engagement but drives higher unsubs despite directly targeting Harvard alumni. Under investigation -- may be audience targeting rather than messaging issue.

7. **Slow creative approval cycles** -- Allison has been consistently slow to review ads. Multiple bumps required (Feb 9, Feb 26 x2, Jan 26 x2). This is a recurring operational blocker -- Mariely frequently waits on approvals to launch new ads.

8. **Delayed subscriber list updates** -- The lookalike audience list hasn't been refreshed in a while despite significant newsletter growth. Stale lookalike data degrades targeting quality.

### Client Sensitivities
- Content involves Harvard financial and legal matters -- flagged as sensitive in Day.ai
- Newsletter covers topics like antisemitism response, governance failures, grade inflation -- creative must handle these topics carefully
- Allison does not want the newsletter positioned as personality-driven or "cult of personality"
