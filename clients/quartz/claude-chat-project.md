## Brand Voice Rules

**NEVER Rules:**
- NEVER use emojis in final creative
- NEVER use alarmist or fear-driven language — "Never alarmist, even when stakes are high"
- NEVER use partisan framing
- NEVER use flashy animations or stock newsroom montages
- NEVER write full paragraphs in video ads — text appears in blocks
- NEVER reuse B-roll across hook variations
- NEVER position Quartz as a facts-only roundup (that is 1440's lane)
- NEVER use casual entertainment-first tone (that is Morning Brew's lane)
- NEVER use dense financial jargon (that is Bloomberg/FT's lane)

**Approved Voice:** Calm, confident, interpretive — slightly skeptical but not cynical. Conversational but intellectually sharp. Assumes the reader is smart, busy, and capable of nuance. "You don't need to panic — you need context." Quartz provides interpretation and context, not just headlines. Credibility and depth without being intimidating.

**Quartz Differentiation (use this in concepts):**

| 1440 | "Facts only" | Interpretation + context |

**Winning Formats:**
- Text Over Video (primary — DCT102 is the benchmark: 205 subs at $2.52 CPL)
- ICP-specific hooks outperform generic ("If you work in finance...", "If you're a CEO, founder...")
- Brand Intro ICP Series ("Hi, we're Quartz" + ICP-specific value prop)
