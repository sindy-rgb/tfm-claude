## Creator Spotlight
**Best frameworks:** Value Inversion, Specificity Ladder, Before-After-Bridge, Funnel-Stage Alignment
**Best hook types:** Curiosity gap (growth clarity), Identity challenge ("Are you a creator who..."), Cognitive dissonance (anti-guru)
**Format priority:** Static (editorial) > Video (TOV w/ blurred faces) > Carousel (never tested) — NO UGC, NO meme formats
**Anti-patterns:** NEVER use Pattern Interrupt (meme/shock variants) or Offer Architecture (urgency/scarcity) — Francis kills anything that feels like performance marketing. "NYT classiness" is the bar.
**Quality signal:** MAR >4 (monthly active readers who open 4+ issues). CPL means nothing if engagement is low.
**Creative volume:** ~5 concepts/sprint
**Nuance:** "Editorial/Native" here means NYT op-ed feel. Text-heavy, zero hype, zero emojis. Confession and growth-clarity angles are the two proven pillars.

## Workweek (5 newsletters)
**Best frameworks:** Social Proof Cascade, Audience Mirror, Problem-Agitate-Solve, Objection-First
**Best hook types:** Identity challenge (role-specific: "If you're a VP in financial services..."), Emotional trigger (vulnerability for IHIH), Cognitive dissonance (anti-guru for TMM)
**Format priority:** UGC (podcast-style talking head) > Static (message-on-glass) > Video (TOV) — carousels untested
**Anti-patterns:** NEVER use polished/corporate brand video aesthetic. NEVER show inconsistent subscriber counts across creative + LP. Each newsletter has its own voice — do not cross-contaminate.
**Quality signal:** V-CAC (Verified Subscriber CAC) per newsletter. Raw CPL is meaningless here.
**Creative volume:** ~5 concepts/sprint across portfolio (prioritize by budget: IHIH/TMM > FTT > Hosp > GTM)
**Nuance:** Five distinct voices. IHIH = vulnerability. FTT = authority gatekeeping. TMM = anti-guru tactical. Hospitalogy = unfair advantage. GTM = operator pain. Broad audiences outperform segmented.

## Insight Links (3 newsletters)

**Creative volume:** ~5 concepts/sprint
**Nuance:** "Teaches how to THINK with AI, not what tools exist." Anti-hype, anti-complexity. Audience is explicitly non-technical — the differentiation from The Neuron/TLDR is conceptual understanding over tool lists. Ruben is very hands-off on creative.
