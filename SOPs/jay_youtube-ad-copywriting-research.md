## 1. Dara Denney — Performance Creative Strategist

**Background:** Senior Director of Performance Creative. Works with DTC brands on paid social. Creator of the Performance Creative Master Course. Claims creative strategy takes up 90% of her time when optimizing Facebook Ads accounts.

### Core Philosophy
- **Creative IS the strategy.** Media buying mechanics matter less than the creative you feed Meta's algorithm.
- **Leave ego at the door.** Test any and all ad formats or messages—what you think will win often doesn't.
- **Data-driven iteration.** Use performance signals (thumbstop ratio, CTR, CVR) to decide what to iterate vs. when to create net-new concepts.

### Key Frameworks

**5 Key Creative Structures (Unicorn Ad Framework):**
1. **UGC Integration** — Authentic customer content for relatability and credibility
2. **Taboo + Aspirational Elements** — Bold, attention-grabbing content that triggers emotional responses (love or hate), creating pattern interruption
3. **Direct Response Editing** — Text movement, close-up framing, organic content contrast
4. **Pre-CTA Engagement Building** — Layer elements that create anticipation before the CTA
5. **Social Proof Leveraging** — Mine comment sections and customer reviews for pain-point language and transformation stories

**3-Part Hook System:**
- Hook must stop the scroll within milliseconds
- Focus on curiosity or relatability before presenting the core message
- Problem-solution dynamics: "Clearly present a problem and position your product as the solution"

**Creative Audit Framework:**
- Track thumbstop ratio (3-sec views / impressions) to identify what stops scrollers
- Track CTR vs. completion rate to diagnose where creative fails
- Track CTR vs. CVR to diagnose message-to-landing-page alignment
- Metrics signal creative fatigue — speed of new creative output is critical

**Performance Creative Master Course Topics:**
- 16 human wants that influence decisions
- Value Prop Mapping
- High-Retention Storyboarding
- Creative Audit Checklist
- Copywriting that converts through compelling primary text

### Iteration vs. New Concept Decision
- **Net new:** Entirely different ad concepts (e.g., celebrity endorsement vs. product promo)
- **Iterations:** Slight variations on existing successful assets (new hooks, different thumbnails, reworked body copy)

### Key Quotes / Insights
- "Making the research process collaborative with different team members opens up a lot more perspectives."
- Brands are now "taking bigger swings" in creative tests — changing up messaging AND images simultaneously to get bigger learnings.
- Text-only ads on Facebook are making a comeback for certain audiences.

## 2. Andrew Faris — Meta Ads Media Buying & Creative Strategy

**Background:** Growing ecommerce brands since 2014. Former head of strategy at Common Thread Collective. CEO of 4x400 (DTC aggregator). Hosts The Andrew Faris Podcast.

### Core Philosophy
- **Creative generates success in two stages:** First it stops the scroll, then it converts. Each stage requires different copy approaches.
- **Volume over perfection.** Build a huge volume of high-performing ads cheaply rather than investing heavily in a few polished pieces.
- **Bad testing methodology is the biggest source of waste.** Most brands over-invest in creative testing campaigns that don't produce actionable learnings.

### Key Frameworks

**The Longform Explainer (Core AJF Playbook Format):**
The ad type that "most consistently outperforms the rest of the account." Four-section structure:

| Section | Purpose | Timing |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **1. The Hook** | Stop the scroll, grab immediate attention | 0:00-0:03 |
| **2. The Education Section** | Provide valuable information, explain the value proposition | 0:03-0:15 |
| **3. Validation & Guarantee** | Build credibility, reduce hesitation with social proof | 0:15-0:25 |
| **4. Offer & CTA** | Present the specific action and drive conversion | 0:25-end |

**Creative Testing Methodology (Revised):**
- Launch ads directly into scale campaigns rather than separate test campaigns
- Run 15-20 ads per ad set — avoid the "learning phase trap"
- Manual bids and target ROAS outperform auto-bid setups
- Watch for false negatives — ads that underperform initially but could win at scale
- Diversify ad formats rather than testing only one type

**7 Meta Ads Tactics He Changed His Mind About:**
- Creative testing campaigns may be the biggest cost center (not revenue driver) in your business
- Launching directly into scale campaigns works better
- Format diversification matters more than copy variation alone

### Newsletter-Applicable Insight
The longform explainer maps directly to newsletter ads:
1. **Hook:** "Most people waste 2 hours a day on news that doesn't matter"
2. **Education:** "Here's what [Newsletter] delivers every morning in 5 minutes"
3. **Validation:** "[X] readers, featured in [publications], trusted by [audience type]"
4. **CTA:** "Subscribe free — join [X]+ readers"

## 3. Savannah Sanchez — UGC & Ad Creative Production at Scale

**Background:** Founded The Social Savannah UGC Ad Agency in 2020. One of the "original UGC creators" (started in 2018). Worked with 300+ brands including Fabletics, Nike, Revolve, BlendJet. Produces 100s of ads per week.

### Core Philosophy
- **Production system > individual genius.** Systematize creative production with detailed briefs, shot lists, and scripts so output scales predictably.
- **Platform-native is everything.** Ads must feel like they belong on the platform — avoid repurposing Facebook content to TikTok or vice versa.
- **Speed of output > polish.** iPhone filming, CapCut editing, compressed weekly cycles. Get data fast.

### Production Workflow
- **Monday-Tuesday:** Shooting (creators film with iPhones using detailed shot lists)
- **Wednesday-Thursday:** Editing (using CapCut)
- **Friday:** New ads delivered to clients (up to 12 per client)
- **Creator team:** 40 recurring creators, <10K followers preferred, trained on her system

### 10 Ad Format Templates (with Copy Tactics)

| Format | Copy Tactic | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Solo Skit** | Build relatable conflict, then product solves it (15-30 sec) | Fashion, Beauty |
| **Greenscreen** | Lead with context/benefit, then feature reveal | Tech, Education |
| **What I Ordered vs Got** | Open with skepticism, close with satisfaction ("I thought...but actually") | Fashion, Subscriptions |
| **Fake Podcast** | Authority voice + surprising statistic + expert-approved positioning | Educational Products |
| **"I Saw This on TikTok"** | FOMO-driven ("Everyone's talking about..."), exclusivity/scarcity | Viral Products |
| **Fun Facts** | Counterintuitive fact hook, position product as the missing link | Health, Apps |
| **3 Reasons Why** | Priority > feature > emotional outcome, parallel structure | All Industries |
| **Comparison** | "Old way vs. New way" framing, one clear transformation metric | Tech, Finance |
| **Car Testimonial** | Conversational tone, natural problem statement, daily routine integration | Lifestyle, Apps |
| **ASMR** | Minimize dialogue, let sound carry emotion, trigger words (smooth, crisp) | Beauty, Food |

### Script Briefing Process
- Writes scripts for creators with specific talking points
- Sends visual examples of desired execution style
- Includes technical specs (e.g., "film at 5 PM for window lighting")
- Provides what to say AND what to avoid
- Key principle: "Show them visually what you're looking for"

### Universal Copy Principles
- **Hook in 2 seconds** with relatable pain point or curiosity trigger
- **Authenticity over polish** — organic feel beats production value
- **Social proof early** — reference trends, endorsements, results
- **Under 30 seconds** — communicate full value prop fast
- **Emotional close** — end with outcome, not feature; emphasize lifestyle integration

## 4. Barry Hott — The Ugly Ads Pioneer

**Background:** $600M+ in social ad spend managed. Brands include AT&T, Toyota, Kraft, Microsoft, True Classic, AG1, Lumin Skin, Harry's Razors, Tushy. Creator of "Building Ads with Barry" cohort program.

### Core Philosophy
- **"People hate ads. They don't hate all ads. They hate ads that FEEL like ads."**
- **If your marketing is too pretty, it probably won't work on paid social.** Polished production triggers the viewer's subconscious "ad blocker."
- **Close Foreplay, stop looking at competitor ads, and spend more time absorbing the native content of Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook.** Match what users actually see in their feeds.

### The Ugly Ads Framework

**What makes an "ugly ad" work:**
- Shaky camera, natural lighting (sun), imperfect audio, lower resolution
- No logo or brand colors in the first frame
- Meme-appropriate fonts (Impact font for memes — any deviation feels "off")
- Feels like content from a friend, not a brand
- Strong narrative and hook underneath the rough aesthetic

**What kills ad performance:**
- Heavy branding in the first frame (logo, custom fonts, brand colors)
- Perfect, overly-polished language
- Heavily scripted reads (use authentic creator voice instead)
- Leading with product features rather than relatable problems
- Mentioning brand name or offer repeatedly (the Burger King "Whopper x3, $5 deal x5" anti-pattern)

**Copy Formula:**
1. Problem focus (relatable pain point)
2. Story/hook (engaging narrative)
3. Authenticity signal (genuine voice, not scripted)
4. Call-to-action (not brand-first)

### Measurable Results
- Ugly ads with strong narratives showed **~30% lift in action intent** over polished alternatives (Lone Ranch Water test)
- The ads that performed better were consistently NOT the more polished ones

### The "Elaborate Heist" Metaphor
"Imagine you're pulling off an elaborate heist, and the subconscious ad blockers are security guards on high alert." Your job is to get the message past the guard by making it look like native content, not an ad.

### Iteration Paralysis Warning (via Dara Denney collab)
New creative strategists get trapped in data-driven iteration loops — recycling winning formulas until they stop winning. Break the cycle by testing genuinely new concepts, not just variations.

## 5. Nick Theriot — Direct Response Copywriting for Meta

**Background:** 10+ years standardizing the marketing process. $100M+ revenue generated for clients at Theriot Solutions. Author of "The Art of Writing Facebook Ads That Scale."

### Core Philosophy
- **90% of advertisers lack three skills:** hook writing, persuasive messaging, and writing ads that scale. Master those three.
- **Show the desired result in the hook.** Don't describe your product — show the outcome the viewer wants.
- **Call out a sub-specific person.** The more specific your audience callout, the stronger the connection.
- **Emotion baiting** — Create strong emotional connections that lead to rapid scaling and profitability.

### The 13 Hook Patterns

| # | Pattern | Example |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Big Claim** | "$10K/month w/ FB ads" |
| 2 | **Fast Claim** | "Double budget overnight" |
| 3 | **Authority** | "I spent $10M on ads..." |
| 4 | **Before/After** | "1x → 3x ROAS" |
| 5 | **Rival Compare** | "3x faster than XYZ" |
| 6 | **No Limits** | "Even w/ no experience" |
| 7 | **Question** | "Who else wants ___?" |
| 8 | **Info** | "How to scale in 2025" |
| 9 | **Newness** | "NEW strategy..." |
| 10 | **Exclusivity** | "The only method that..." |
| 11 | **Belief Flip** | "I thought ads were a scam until..." |
| 12 | **Solution Callout** | "Don't spend another $ until..." |
| 13 | **Direct Callout** | "To the person who's tried everything..." |

### Script Hierarchy
Nick's ad hierarchy: **1. Text Hook → 2. Script → 3. Visual Hook**
The text hook (on-screen text) is the most important element, followed by the script (what the person says), followed by the visual hook (what they see).

### Ad Script Structure
1. **Hook** — Show the desired result or call out a specific person
2. **Flow into the product** — Transition naturally from the hook to what you're offering
3. **Build the case** — How amazing the experience/product was, the results achieved
4. **Handle objections** — Address why someone might hesitate
5. **Close with offer** — "Save 60% off today" or "Start [desired outcome] today if you click below"

### Emotion Baiting Technique
- Put a text caption on screen: "POV: You finally [achieved desired result] thanks to [product/method]"
- Show short video clips of someone in the desired state
- The viewer emotionally projects themselves into the outcome

### 6-Part Ad Creation System
1. **Market Research** — 5 levels of customer psychology research
2. **Product Positioning** — Differentiate in competitive markets
3. **Hook Writing** — 3 essential elements for scroll-stopping hooks
4. **Persuasion Mechanics** — Strategic copy that overcomes resistance
5. **Objection Handling** — Address conversion-blocking concerns
6. **Performance Diagnosis** — Identify and fix underperforming ads

### Newsletter-Applicable Hook Patterns
For newsletter ads, the most relevant patterns:
- **Authority:** "Read by 175,000+ CEOs and founders"
- **Info:** "How to stay ahead of [industry] in 5 minutes/day"
- **Question:** "Who else wants to stop missing [key trend]?"
- **Direct Callout:** "To the busy professional who's tired of information overload..."
- **Belief Flip:** "I used to think newsletters were dead until..."
- **Exclusivity:** "The only daily briefing that [specific benefit]"

## 6. Newsletter-Specific Ad Copy Best Practices (Cross-Creator + Industry)

### The Core Problem
"Sign up for our newsletter" isn't an offer — it's a chore. People's inboxes are already flooded. Treat your newsletter like a product with real value.

### Newsletter Ad Copy Formula
1. **Hook:** Pain point or curiosity gap about the reader's information needs
2. **Value prop:** What they get, how often, in how many minutes
3. **Social proof:** Subscriber count + audience description (not just a number)
4. **Incentive:** Lead magnet (checklist, guide, mini-course) in exchange for subscribe
5. **CTA:** Clear, low-friction ("Subscribe free" > "Sign up for our newsletter")

### Social Proof Best Practice
- **Strong:** "Join 175,000+ Investment Bankers, Investors, and VCs"
- **Weak:** "75,000 readers"
- **For small lists (<1,000):** Lead with expertise or credentials, not subscriber count

### 7 High-Converting Newsletter Creative Types
1. **Memes** — Show subscription benefits with humor/attention (not necessarily funny)
2. **Founder Images** — Put a face to the brand
3. **Notes/Notion Screenshots** — Stand out with notepad-style visuals
4. **Carousels** — Feature top headlines or past stories
5. **Press Features** — Build credibility through publication mentions
6. **Testimonial Screenshots** — User-generated social proof
7. **Authority/Social Proof** — Display credentials or recognition

### Quality Over Cost
"A $3 lead that sticks is better than a $0.80 one that churns." The best-performing ad may not be the cheapest — it's the one that attracts people who stay.

### Lead Form Optimization
- **Headline:** Benefit-driven (what subscribers gain)
- **Sub-headline:** Newsletter topic + social proof + "free" positioning
- **Form fields:** Email + name only to start; each additional field decreases CVR
- **Privacy:** Visible policy links reduce friction
- **Instant value:** Immediate delivery of lead magnet on confirmation

### Platform Notes
- 85% of users watch video without sound — burn-in captions are non-negotiable
- Vertical (9:16) ad formats for mobile-first
- Facebook is a better testing ground than TikTok — winning FB videos often scale well to TikTok

## Synthesis: Common Themes Across All Creators

### 1. The Hook Is Everything
Every single creator prioritizes the first 1-3 seconds. Whether it's Theriot's 13 hook patterns, Denney's 3-Part Hook System, or Faris's longform explainer hook section — the consensus is absolute: **if you don't stop the scroll, nothing else matters.**

### 2. Authenticity Beats Production Value
Barry Hott's ugly ads philosophy is the most extreme version, but every creator agrees: native-feeling content outperforms polished brand creative. Savannah Sanchez films everything on iPhones. Dara Denney tracks thumbstop ratio, which rewards organic-looking content. Andrew Faris advocates volume over polish.

### 3. Problem-First, Product-Second
All five creators lead with the viewer's problem or desired outcome, not the product:
- Theriot: "Show the desired result in the hook"
- Hott: "Problem focus → Story → Authenticity → CTA"
- Denney: "Clearly present a problem and position your product as the solution"
- Faris: Hook → Education → Validation → Offer (product comes last)
- Sanchez: "Open with skepticism, close with satisfaction"

### 4. Volume and Speed of Testing
- Sanchez produces 100s of ads per week with a compressed Mon-Fri cycle
- Faris runs 15-20 ads per ad set and advocates launching into scale campaigns
- Denney emphasizes speed because "creatives fatigue so quickly"
- The consensus: produce more creative faster rather than perfecting fewer ads

### 5. Specific Audience Callouts
- Theriot's "call out a sub-specific person" is the most explicit version
- Denney's UGC integration creates relatability through specific audience mirroring
- Sanchez's format templates (car testimonial, solo skit) create situational specificity
- For newsletters: naming the audience type ("CEOs," "busy professionals," "investors") dramatically outperforms generic language

### 6. Emotion Over Logic
- Theriot's "emotion baiting" technique creates emotional projection into desired outcomes
- Hott's ugly ads work because they feel genuine and relatable (emotional trust)
- Denney identifies "16 human wants" that influence decisions
- Faris's education section builds emotional investment before asking for action

### 7. Social Proof Is Non-Negotiable
Every creator integrates social proof, but the format varies:
- Subscriber/reader counts with audience descriptors
- Testimonial screenshots
- Press feature mentions
- Comment section mining
- "I saw this on TikTok" FOMO triggers

## TFM Application: Newsletter Ad Copy Playbook

### Recommended Hook Patterns for Newsletter Clients
Based on the research, these hook patterns map best to newsletter growth:

| Hook Type | Template | Source |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Authority + Specificity | "Read by [X]+ [specific audience type]" | Theriot #3 |
| Pain Point + Solution | "Stop wasting [time] on [problem]. Get [benefit] in [time]" | Denney Problem-Solution |
| FOMO + Social Proof | "[X]+ [audience] already get this every [frequency]" | Sanchez "I Saw This" |
| Belief Flip | "I used to think [common objection] until I found [newsletter]" | Theriot #11 |
| Ugly/Native | Meme format, Notes screenshot, or casual selfie-style talking head | Hott Ugly Ads |
| Longform Explainer | Hook → What you get → Who reads it → Subscribe free | Faris 4-Section |

### Recommended Testing Cadence
- **Weekly:** 4-6 new hook variations per client (matching DCT 4-3-2-2 structure)
- **Bi-weekly:** 2-3 new format types (rotate between meme, founder image, UGC, notes screenshot)
- **Monthly:** 1 net-new concept (entirely different angle, not an iteration)

### Copy Quality Checklist (Pre-Launch)
- [ ] Hook stops the scroll in <2 seconds
- [ ] Problem/pain point stated before product mention
- [ ] Specific audience called out (not generic)
- [ ] Social proof included with audience descriptor
- [ ] CTA is clear and low-friction
- [ ] No heavy branding in first frame (if video)
- [ ] Feels native to platform (would you scroll past it as an ad?)
- [ ] Subscriber count verified against ESP (per QA SOP)
- [ ] Publish frequency verified (per QA SOP)
- [ ] No fabricated statistics (per QA SOP)
